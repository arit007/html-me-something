Weird Animals I Like

Axolotl

The axolotl (pronounced ACK-suh-LAH-thul) is a salamander but it is also known as the Mexican Walking Fish. It is currently critically endangered. Since 2010, axolotls in the wild have been near extinction because of the urbanization in Mexico City and water pollution to the nearby lakes they live in. They are also sold as food in Mexican markets. Axolotls are used as a subject in scientific research due to them being able to regenerate lost limbs.

Pangolin

Pangolins are the only scaled mammals in existence. They are able to capture ants and termites with their long tongues. They have protective keratin scales that cover theri skin. Pangolins are the most trafficked mammals in the world. Four species are vulnerable, 2 are endangered, and another 2 are critically endangered.

Blobfish

Blobfish are a type of deep sea fish that lives off the coast of Australia and New Zealand. The blobfish is a gelatinous mass that a density that is slightly less than water. Having no muscle is not a concern as they simply swallow endible matter that float in front of them. They seem bulbous when on land yet take on the appearance of its relative the bony fish when in water.

Proboscis Monkey

The male proboscis monkey use their enourmous noses to attract mates. They are native to the jungles of Borneo and live near rivers, mangroves, and swamps. Most time is spent living in trees and only move on land when in search of food to eat. Proboscis monkeys are also the primate world's best swimmers.

Tarsier

Each eye of the tarsier is as big as its brain. All tarsier species are nocturnal but are sometimes active during the day as well. All are especially adapted to leaping which is due to their long hind limbs. Young tarsiers are born with fur, big eyes, and are able to climb within a day of birth.
